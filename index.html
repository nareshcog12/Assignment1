Exploring the Red Planet: Mars

Mars, often called the "Red Planet," is the fourth planet from the Sun and the second-smallest planet in the Solar System, being larger than only Mercury. In English, Mars carries the name of the Roman god of war and is often referred to as the Red Planet because of the reddish iron oxide prevalent on its surface, giving it a striking appearance from Earth.

Scientists are fascinated by Mars due to its potential for past or present life, and its similarities to Earth. Evidence suggests that Mars once had liquid water on its surface, including rivers, lakes, and even an ocean. Future missions aim to further investigate these possibilities and potentially pave the way for human exploration.
